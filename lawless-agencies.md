<h2 id="content">The Threat Lawlessness Poses</h2>
<p>Citizens hold a society together with shared values and shared expectations of behavior. We make laws to reflect and specify these rules. People can disagree with laws and argue to change them, and this is why we have a set of laws that specifically deals with that process. When certain individuals or groups flagrantly disregard the laws and routinely get away with it, however, public trust in society deteriorates. Over time, such double standards can transform what was once a stable country into a free-for-all, where the only certainty is in whatever you can get away with. Already, we're beginning to see the effects of this deterioration.
	<br /><br />
  The most immediate effect of government lawlessness is injury and poverty, something we've been witnessing firsthand ever since the start of this administration. The mass illegal firings of federal agents working at vital government offices have resulted in a widespread lack of responsive action, program / funding availability, core task delegation / completion, and even basic information about incredibly important aspects of life. These have been felt hardest in Social Security, Medicare / Medicaid, Veterans' Affairs, FEMA natural disaster relief, and FAA flight safety. People have suffered and even died from preventable medical and safety problems stemming directly from these staffing cuts. Properties have been lost due to failings in both financial disbursements and severe weather management. Many Americans' financial stability has been completely destroyed. And this isn't even touching on how the illegal suppression of funding has only compounded these problems further. All of this happened because the standard procedures that are supposed to be in place to ensure safe transitions were <b>not</b> followed. Had these firings and cuts been performed in the correct manner proscribed by law, none of these government organizations would've experienced any of the sudden and unexpected interruptions in staffing or resources required for them to perform their jobs safely and effectively. The result: harm and panic on a national scale, all because certain individuals in this administration wanted to be above the law.
	<br /><br />
  As reports of these harms spread, another side effect emerges: economic and social instability. Companies make changes to their hiring and pricing practices to reflect what they view as a less survivable environment, increasing financial pressures on ordinary Americans. This, in turn, pushes private citizens into more desperate acts that can compound the situation even further. Firings and reductions in hiring forces some less well-off Americans to sell what few assets they have at subpar prices just to get by, and even pushes the more impoverished into black market crime just to make ends meet. What's worse, the increased stress will flat-out result in more interpersonal crimes as tempers and composures break. Rising prices of goods and crumbling investment values also contribute to worsening these outcomes. This is all only compounded further when a runaway administration unilaterally enacts massive tariffs, which have a severe and immediate amplifying effect on everything just described. When individual citizens and business organizations both bear witness to an entire branch of federal government that refuses to reign in an out-of-bounds administration, this only makes the economic and social instability even worse.
	<br /><br />
  Perhaps the absolute worst effect of all, however, is the erosion of the peoples' trust that justice still exists. Rampant tyrannical behavior is bad enough for a country's social cohesion; however, when there's a sense that the system is at least still fair, that acts as a vital anchor in what's otherwise a sea of unpredictability and chaos. What we've seen here, however, is that the current administration is also corrupt to its core. Presidential pardons, once a tool that was supposed to be used only sparingly and with great deliberation, are now being handed out left and right as political favors. In some cases, it even seems as if they're being sold for acceptably large sums of money. Even worse is the thoughtless and knee-jerk pattern of lawsuits and arrests taking place at every perceived offense, regardless of the actual gravity or legality of the action in question. Many inoccuous acts that have been legal for decades, if not centuries, are now being painted as treason and terrorism. Constitutionally-protected rights of anyone who isn't in line with the current regime are being trampled on daily, whereas the actual crimes and harms committed by anyone who falls in line or pays tribute are dismissed categorically. What we're seeing at the highest levels of government doesn't resemble fairness or justice in even the most remote sense. Instead, it resembles favoritism and hypocrisy that we previously thought possible only in the worst historical examples of dictatorships.
	<br /><br />
  The solution here is perhaps the easiest and hardest of all the issues described so far: <b>replace the existing spineless lackeys with representatives who will actually do their Constitutionally-appointed jobs</b>.</p>

		<h3>We Can Still Salvage This</h3>
		<p>Our federal debt is at $37 trillion. Our deficit is at $1.8 trillion. Out of the $14.7 trillion in income earned by taxpayers, the IRS collected $2.2 trillion of that as revenue. Furthermore, the total value of all wealth held in America sits at around $160 trillion. Put simply: we have a number of ways we can remedy this situation before it gets truly out of hand.</p>
	</div>
	<div class="6u$ 12u$(small)">
		<h3>Reducing Debt Also Reduces Deficit</h3>
		<p>As illustrated by the example above, the actual total amount of debt also influences how quickly it grows. Currently, the interest accrued on our debt is $1.1 trillion every year. If we were to cut our total debt in half, that would also cut the interest gains in half. This, in turn, reduces how much funding we need to cut for vital programs that help the vast majority of Americans.</p>
	</div>

	<div class="4u 12u$(medium)">
		<h3>Large Budget Changes Are to Blame</h3>
		<p>The reality is, our debt didn't balloon out of control because of comparatively tiny expenditures. The cause of our current problem is right there in our nation's revenue and spending history. The 2017 Tax Cuts act contributed $450 billion to our deficit. Excessive payments to Private Military Contractors (who earn between 2 to 4 times as much as actual military service members) contributes another $300 to $450 billion to our deficit. These are among the largest offenders that have arisen in recent years.</p>
